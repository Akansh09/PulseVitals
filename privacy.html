Privacy policy

Last updated: 2026-04-18 · Version 1

Summary

PulseVitals is a browser extension that measures the performance of pages you visit. It runs on your device only. It does not send your data to any server. It never has.

What we collect

When you have an active tab open and the extension is installed, PulseVitals observes the browser's built-in performance APIs for that tab. We read:

  • Navigation timing (navigationStart → domComplete).
  • Paint timing and Core Web Vitals (first-paint, first-contentful-paint, largest-contentful-paint, CLS, INP).
  • Resources your page loads (URL, transfer size, duration, initiator type) via performance.getEntriesByType("resource").
  • Storage usage estimate for the active tab's origin via navigator.storage.estimate(), plus byte counts of localStorage and sessionStorage.
  • On-page SEO signals (<title>, <meta name="description">, <h1> count, canonical URL, Open Graph image, viewport meta, JSON-LD) when the SEO checks feature is enabled.
  • Accessibility violations from a bundled minimal axe-core rule set when the a11y score feature is enabled.

What we store

Measurement snapshots are stored locally using Chrome's built-in storage APIs:

  • chrome.storage.local — up to 10 snapshots per origin you visit; roughly 2 KB per snapshot. Used for the history view and RUM histograms.
  • chrome.storage.sync — your Options preferences (auto-measure toggle, mobile-default toggle, platform tips toggle, denylist, budgets). Roams across Chrome profiles signed into your Google account.

Everything above lives on your device (and, for sync data, Chrome's syncing infrastructure managed by Google under their own policy).

What we do not do

  • No data is sent to any PulseVitals server. There is no PulseVitals server.
  • No telemetry. We do not know whether you installed, uninstalled, or used the extension.
  • No crash reporting. If it crashes on your machine, we do not find out.
  • No third-party analytics, tag managers, advertising pixels, or A/B testing libraries are loaded by the extension.
  • No cross-origin reads: the content script reads performance APIs for the active tab's own origin, nothing else.

What leaves your device

Only when you explicitly act:

  • Export writes a JSON and CSV to your Downloads folder.
  • Share writes a self-contained HTML report to your Downloads folder. Whether you email or upload that file is your decision.

Permissions

Each Chrome permission the extension requests is justified in docs/permissions.md in the source repository. The permission list is: storage, activeTab, tabs, and (optionally, when you enable budget alerts) notifications.

Your controls

  • The Options page has a Clear history button that removes every stored snapshot.
  • Uninstalling the extension removes all locally-stored data.
  • Disabling auto-measure from Options stops capture without uninstalling.
  • The denylist in Options prevents measurement on specific origins entirely.

Changes to this policy

Material changes will be announced in the extension's "What's new" card on the next version update, and the Last updated date above will change.

Contact

For privacy questions, open an issue via the Report an issue link in the extension popup.
